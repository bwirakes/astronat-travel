### 2c. Critical finding — the app is computing the **wrong** ASC

The lens claims Brandon becomes **Pisces** rising in Dubai. This is **not** the
relocated natal Ascendant — it's the **geodetic** ASC of Dubai itself.

Trace:

- `personal-lens.ts` calls `computeRelocatedAscLon(destLat, destLon)`.
- That helper lives in `app/lib/geodetic.ts` and implements the formula
  `(mcLonDeg − 90 + dOA) mod 360` where `mcLonDeg = destLon` and `dOA` is
  derived purely from the destination latitude.
- **That formula has no dependency on the user's birth date or birth time.**
  It is the geodetic ASC formula (time-invariant), misnamed
  `computeRelocatedAscLon`.

The correct "relocated natal ASC" — the sign the user rises under at Dubai —
comes from Swiss Ephemeris `swe.houses(jd_natal, destLat, destLon, sys)` and
reading `ascmc[0]`. `lib/readings/astrocarto.ts` already does it properly
(see `relocatedCuspsAt`). The weather runner does not.

**Impact:** chart-ruler line, ruler-journey diagram, rulerJourneyChain, and
the hook's "your chart ruler, Jupiter…" line are all derived from the wrong
Ascendant. They are currently describing *Dubai's geodetic rising sign*, not
*Brandon's rising sign at Dubai*.

### 2d. What it *should* say

25.23°N — 51° west and 31° north. Moving west rotates the Local Sidereal Time

Roughly, Brandon's relocated ASC at Dubai is plausibly around **late
Aries / early Taurus / very late Pisces** depending on exact LST at his natal
instant. Only Swiss Ephemeris can give the right answer.

The PDF does not give us a precise check value for Dubai, but we can state
